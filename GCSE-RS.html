✍️ Exam Technique
⏱ Time Management — Know Your Paper
The Golden Rule — works for BOTH papers

1 minute per mark

Islam: 102 marks = 105 min ✓  |  Christianity: 102 marks = 105 min ✓
Both papers are identical in structure and timing. One rule works for both.

☪️ Islam Paper 1C — 1 hr 45 min · 102 marks · 4 questions
Q1 & Q3 (a) 3 · (b) 4 · (c) 5 · (d) 15 + 3 SPaG 27 min each
Q2 & Q4 (a) 3 · (b) 4 · (c) 5 · (d) 12 marks 24 min each

Q1 and Q3 carry SPaG marks — use specialist terms carefully. Q2 and Q4 do not, but still write well. Leaving any question blank loses marks that are easy to pick up.

✝️ Christianity Paper 2B — 1 hr 45 min · 102 marks · 4 questions
Q1 & Q3 (a) 3 · (b) 4 · (c) 5 · (d) 15 + 3 SPaG 27 min each
Q2 & Q4 (a) 3 · (b) 4 · (c) 5 · (d) 12 marks 24 min each

Same structure as Islam. Q1 and Q3 carry SPaG marks — use specialist terms carefully. The 1 minute per mark rule applies to both papers equally.

✓ Review Checklist — use remaining time after each question
1 Part D — SOWA: Is there a direct quote with reference in the body? If not, add one now.
2 Part D — Conclusion: Does it say WHY one side is stronger, not just which side?
3 Part D — Evaluation: Are evaluative phrases (convincing, weakened by, stronger because) in the body — not just the conclusion?
4 Part C — SOWA: Is there a named source with reference? This is the 5th mark — easy to forget.
5 SPaG questions: Have I used 4-5 specialist terms correctly? (Tawhid, Al-Qadr, Tahrif, Akhirah, Risalah...)
Part D Paragraph Formula — P.E.E.E.
Use this structure for EACH paragraph
P
Point — state your argument clearly. "Some Muslims agree because..."
E
Evidence — use a quote here, in the body, not at the end. "This is supported by the Qur'an: '...' (ref)"
E
Explain — say what the quote means and why it supports your point.
E
Evaluate — judge the strength of this argument. "This is convincing because... / However, this is weakened by..."

Example: "Some Muslims disagree with the statement because Allah has given humans free will and responsibility. This is supported by the Qur'an: 'Allah does not change the condition of a people until they change what is in themselves' (13:11). This shows that human action matters — if outcomes were fixed, this verse would be meaningless. This is a convincing argument because it directly undermines the claim that being a good Muslim is pointless."

Replace "This Means That"
Vary your connectives — don't use the same phrase more than once
❌ "This means that..." ✓ This suggests... / This implies...
❌ "This means that..." (evaluating) ✓ This is convincing because... / This strengthens the argument that...
❌ "Therefore some people agree..." ✓ On balance, this supports the view that... / As a result...
❌ "This can be strengthened as..." ✓ Furthermore... / This is reinforced by... / Consequently...
❌ "This shows that..." (repeated) ✓ This demonstrates... / This highlights... / This reveals...
Agree / Disagree Vocabulary

In Part D, avoid repeating "I agree" or "I disagree." Use these instead to sound sophisticated and pick up language marks.

✓ To AGREE / support the statement ✗ To DISAGREE / challenge it
This is a strong argument because...However, this is a weakness because...
This is convincing as it shows...This fails to explain why...
This is persuasive evidence that...A key challenge to this view is...
This is significant because...This is inconsistent with...
This view is supported by...This is limited because it ignores...
This is a powerful point since...Many would find this unconvincing as...
This demonstrates clearly that...The main problem with this view is...
This is particularly relevant today because...This contradicts the teaching that...
Justified Conclusion Formula
Structure to use every time
1
"In conclusion, looking at both sides of the argument..." — signal you are concluding.
2
"The strongest argument is [FOR / AGAINST] because..." — state your position clearly.
3
Give ONE reason why that side is stronger — link to a teaching or source of authority.
4
"However, [other side] is also important because..." — show you understand the nuance.
5
"Therefore, overall I [agree/disagree] with the statement." — land the conclusion firmly.

Example: "In conclusion, looking at both sides, the strongest argument is against the statement, because the Prophet himself called Greater Jihad 'the greater struggle' — suggesting the inner battle is more central to Muslim life. However, the Qur'an's permission of defensive war is also important. Therefore, overall I disagree with the statement."

Part C Answer Structure
5 marks = 2 developed points + 1 source of authority
1
Point: State the belief/practice clearly.
2
Develop: Explain WHY it matters or HOW it affects believers — add detail.
3
Source of Authority: Quote or reference the Qur'an / Bible / Hadith directly. This is the 5th mark.
4
Second point + develop: New idea, not a repeat of the first.

You do NOT need a source for the second point — just one in the whole answer.

Useful Connecting Phrases
For introducing evidence

"This is supported by..."  ·  "As the Qur'an states..."  ·  "According to..."  ·  "This teaching is shown in..."  ·  "This is demonstrated by the fact that..."  ·  "From a [Muslim/Christian/non-religious] perspective..."
